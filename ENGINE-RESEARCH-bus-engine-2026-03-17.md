# SECTION 2: Best-in-Class UX for B2G Transit Websites

## What Procurement Officers Need Before Making Contact

Government procurement officers are risk-averse decision-makers accountable to committees. They do NOT want to call and be educated — they arrive at a call already convinced.

**Must-have pre-contact elements:**
1. **Compliance documentation** (immediately findable, not buried): Buy America certs, FMVSS certs, DBE program status, FTA-approved TVM list inclusion (hard gate — agencies cannot award FTA-assisted contracts to TVMs not on this list), SAM.gov registration, business certifications
2. **Past performance evidence**: Named agency references with dollar values and dates, contract numbers, delivery performance metrics (on-time %, defect rates), agency logos
3. **Pricing signals**: "Cooperative purchasing available — no new RFP required" flagged prominently, financing/lease options
4. **Technical credibility**: Certifications (ISO, IATF, state-specific), facility specs (sq footage, bays, throughput capacity), staffing counts and qualifications

## Technical Spec Presentation (Best Practice)

**Wrong:** Lead with specs. "We replace drivetrain components."
**Right:** Layer it:
- **Layer 1 (above the fold):** Outcome language — "Extends bus useful life 4–8 years." "Passes FTA post-delivery audit on first inspection." TCO comparison vs. new purchase.
- **Layer 2 (downloadable):** Model-by-model spec sheets in PDF, bill of materials showing domestic vs. imported content (Buy America math), FTA bus testing certification reports (49 CFR Part 665), FMVSS compliance matrices
- **Layer 3 (interactive):** Spec configurator, refurb vs. replace comparison table

## Compelling B2G Case Study Structure (600–900 words or 2-page PDF)

```
Header: [Agency Name] — [State] — [Year]
Subhead: One-sentence outcome with a number

Section 1 — The Challenge (100 words)
- Agency size (ridership, fleet count)
- Problem: aging fleet, budget constraint, compliance deadline
- Why new purchase was not viable

Section 2 — The Solution (150 words)
- Scope of work with specificity (buses, systems)
- Timeline from award to delivery
- Compliance challenges navigated

Section 3 — The Results (100 words)
- Cost savings vs. new bus purchase (dollars and %)
- Useful life extension (years)
- Compliance outcomes (passed pre-award/post-delivery audit, first time)
- Ridership impact if available

Section 4 — Quote (attributed with name, title, agency)

Section 5 — Fast Facts sidebar
- Contract value, buses completed, timeline, certifications
```

**Critical data points government buyers need:** dollar amount, timeline, first-time audit pass rate, compliance items addressed.

## Winning FTA/Buy America/DBE Compliance Page Structure

**Page design standard:** Each item needs (1) regulatory citation, (2) plain-English compliance statement, (3) downloadable certificate link, (4) compliance contact.

**Buy America (49 USC 5323(j) / 49 CFR Part 661):**
- Statement of 70%+ domestic component compliance
- Final assembly location (must be U.S.)
- Pre-award Buy America Certificate available upon request
- Component/subcomponent documentation readiness statement
- Waiver history (transparency builds trust)

**FTA Transit Vehicle Manufacturer Status:**
- Confirmation of inclusion on FTA Eligible TVM List
- DBE goal submission status (2025 interim rule — annual submission temporarily paused; maintain by emailing FTATVMSubmissions@dot.gov)

**DBE Program (49 CFR Part 26 / 26.49):**
- Annual DBE goal percentage and methodology
- DBE subcontractor opportunities contact
- If large business (not DBE-certified): DBE goal, subcontracting opportunities, contact

**ADA/Section 504 (49 CFR Parts 27, 37, 38):**
- Statement all vehicles meet 49 CFR Part 38 accessibility specs
- Lift/ramp, securement, doorway clearance (32" minimum) compliance
- Maintenance commitment language

**FMVSS (49 CFR Part 571 / Part 665):**
- Self-certification statement per NHTSA standards
- FTA bus testing certification available upon request

**CARB (California-specific):**
- ICT regulation compliance posture
- ZEPS zero-emission positioning (ICT-compliant conversion service)

**SAM.gov:** UEI = QN7UN15K9NP2 | CAGE = 1QA89 | Expiry Aug 25, 2026

# SECTION 3: The Unified Record — Transit Bus in Production

## What AssetWorks Calls the Record

**"Unit"** is the canonical record in FleetFocus. Everything anchors to the Unit Number.

**Core fields always present:**
- Unit Number (internal fleet ID), VIN, Year/Make/Model, Fuel Type
- Seating Capacity (total + ADA), ADA Accessibility (lift/ramp type)
- In-Service Date, Ownership Type, Funding Source
- Useful Life Benchmark (FTA: 12 years / 500,000 miles for standard transit bus)
- Odometer / Primary Meter, Engine Hours

**NTD A-30 Required Fields (FTA Annual Revenue Vehicle Inventory):**
- Vehicle type code, vehicle length, year of last rebuild/overhaul
- Annual vehicle miles/revenue miles, average lifetime miles
- Emergency contingency flag, dedicated/non-dedicated use

**Financial/Compliance Fields:**
- Acquisition cost, in-service date, estimated service life (months + meters)
- License/permit renewal dates, inspection due dates, warranty terms
- Department/Location, Depot/Garage assignment

## Mobile Presentation for Floor Technicians (Industry Standard)

**Convergent pattern (RTA Fleet360, AMCS, Fleetio Go):**
- Technician logs in on tablet or ruggedized phone
- WO queue sorted by priority/due date
- Each WO shows: unit number, bus description, fault/complaint, estimated hours
- Tap to open WO: full vehicle history inline (last service, open issues, warranty status)
- Clock in/out directly on WO — records actual labor time
- Parts lookup: scan barcode or search, pull from parts inventory
- Photo attachment: camera or live capture, attached to WO
- Labor + parts lines added as work progresses
- Complete and sign — digital signature or PIN
- WO closes, meter reading updated, costs posted automatically

**Design principles:**
- Large touch targets (44x44px minimum — gloved-hand use)
- Max 3 taps to any action
- Offline capability (syncs when reconnected)
- Voice notes for technician comments (BusCMMS)
- Barcode/QR scanning for bus ID and parts
- No paper — WO prints only for customer copy or audit

## 6-Month Refurbishment Timeline (Activity Feed Pattern)

**Phase 1 — Intake & Inspection (Days 1–7)**
- Acceptance inspection + condition documented + photos taken
- Pre-work inspection sheet transmitted to agency
- WO opened in CMMS
- Contract and scope confirmed against bus condition

**Phase 2 — Disassembly (Week 2–3)**
- Complete teardown: exterior panels, interior stripped, powertrain removed, suspension removed, HVAC pulled, electrical harnesses inspected

**Phase 3 — Structural/Body Assessment (Week 3–4)**
- Inner structure exposed + inspected for corrosion
- Defective structural members identified + photos sent to agency
- Corroded sections cut out, new metal fabricated and welded
- Undercarriage sandblasted, treated, undercoated

**Phase 4 — Mechanical Rebuild (Weeks 4–7)**
- Engine repower/overhaul, transmission, suspension, brakes, steering, wheelchair lift rebuild

**Phase 5 — Systems Reinstallation (Weeks 6–9)**
- Electrical harnesses, HVAC, flooring, seats, interior panels, ceiling, handrails, driver's compartment

**Phase 6 — Body & Paint (Weeks 8–10)**
- Body panels, primer, paint booth with agency livery, decals, ADA markings

**Phase 7 — QC & Pre-Delivery (Weeks 10–12)**
- Quality control inspection against contract spec
- Road test (speed, gradeability, fuel economy, noise, accessibility, accessories)
- Punch list resolved
- FTA documentation package assembled: Buy America cert, post-delivery purchaser's requirements cert, FMVSS cert, resident inspector report
- Final photos

**Phase 8 — Delivery**
- Bus delivered / agency pickup
- Documentation package handed over
- Warranty period begins (typically 12 months parts/labor on overhauled components)

# SECTION 4: Agency Portal Standards

## ⭐ CONFIRMED: No Agency Portal Exists in the Market

Research confirmed no transit fleet software and no refurbishment company offers an agency-facing portal for tracking buses in production. The closest analogue is:
- **Decisiv DSMP:** Fleet managers monitor service cases across service providers in real time — but commercial-truck-first, requires network, no transit refurb focus
- **Buildertrend Client Portal:** Construction homeowners track project schedules, progress photos, milestone approvals — this is the functional analogue for bus refurbishment

**This is a competitive moat feature. First to build it wins.**

## What Transit Agency Fleet Managers Actually Want

Based on APTA procurement guidance, FTA TAM planning requirements, and fleet manager pain points:

1. **Real-time production status per bus** — fleet managers managing a batch of 20 buses cannot call 20 times. One dashboard showing each bus's current stage.
2. **Photo documentation by stage** — intake photos (pre-existing damage protection), progress photos, delivery photos. Protects both parties in disputes and FTA Triennial Review audits.
3. **Compliance document access before delivery** — Buy America, FMVSS, purchaser's certs. Under FTA rules, must be in agency's file before revenue service. Currently received at delivery. Earlier + electronic = differentiator.
4. **Change order visibility** — unexpected scope changes need formal approval trail, not email chains. FTA-funded overhauls have compliance implications for change orders.
5. **Schedule confidence** — FTA grant funds tied to project timelines. Fleet managers need per-bus projected return-to-service dates.
6. **Single contact/messaging thread** — per-bus conversation replacing email chains.
7. **NTD data handoff** — post-overhaul data formatted for import into AssetWorks/Trapeze (A-30 "year of last rebuild" update).

## Auth Model for B2B Portal Serving Government Agencies

**Primary: Magic Link (email-based passwordless)**
- Government agencies whitelist email domains — magic links to `.gov` or transit agency domains are trusted
- No password management burden for infrequent users (fleet managers log in when notified, not daily)
- Works with any email client, no app install required
- ⭐ Magic links are purpose-built for "infrequent login, project-based" use cases

**Secondary (Phase 2): SAML/OIDC SSO**
- Metro-scale agencies (WMATA, MTA, LA Metro) run Okta or Azure AD
- Enterprise SSO lets their IT provision/deprovision without contacting CCW
- Clerk handles both magic links and enterprise SSO natively

**Avoid:** Username/password (fleet managers forget passwords on monthly-visit portals), consumer OAuth (government IT blocks Google/Microsoft personal OAuth)

- All buses currently in production at CCW
- Each bus as a card: unit number, model, current stage, estimated return date, days in shop
- Color-coded status: green (on schedule), yellow (attention needed), red (delayed / needs approval)
- Aggregate: X of Y buses complete, projected delivery dates

**Page 2 — ⭐ Bus Detail View (the money page)**

## Additional California-Specific Compliance

- **CARB In-Use Transit Bus Regulation:** emission requirements for existing fleet operations by fuel type and model year. Agencies operating diesel buses in CA have CARB compliance obligations — CCW's ZEPS program is a direct solution.
- **HVIP Vouchers:** Fuel Cell Electric Buses qualify for $300,000 CARB HVIP vouchers. Battery Electric Buses qualify for HVIP incentives. CCW/ZEPS should prominently feature HVIP on the ZEPS page.
- **California Vehicle Code:** Title registration requirements for buses sold/transferred in CA. Commercial vehicle title transfers require weight certification.
- **BAR (Bureau of Automotive Repair):** Smog inspection — typically exempt for transit agencies but relevant for used bus sales to non-exempt buyers (TSI sales to private operators, charter companies, schools).

## Fleet Disposal / Used Bus Title Compliance (TSI-Specific)

- All FTA-funded vehicles carry federal interest until useful life is reached
- State DOTs (as FTA pass-through grantees) are named as lien holders
- Before title freely transfers: state DOT must release lien
- **Practical steps for TSI used bus purchases:**
  1. Request FTA federal interest status from selling agency
  2. Confirm useful life met (mileage, age, condition-based)
  3. Obtain lien release from state DOT / TPA
  4. Complete state DMV title transfer
  5. Document chain of title for CCW's own Buy America and compliance records
