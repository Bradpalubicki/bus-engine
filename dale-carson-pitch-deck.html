Finding 1.1
Divi JS Rendering — All Content Invisible to Google
Critical

Divi renders the entire homepage in JavaScript. Google sees a blank shell — no headings, no body text, no semantic structure. CCW's 38 years of expertise and services are invisible to every search engine.

Business Impact

A transit agency searching "bus refurbishment contractor" or "ZEPS electric bus conversion" will not find CCW. The nation's largest refurbisher ranks for zero non-branded queries.

Finding 1.2
No Sitemap — "Coming Soon" Zombie Page Is Indexed by Google
Critical

/sitemap.xml returns 404. A "New Site Coming Soon" page is live and indexed — signaling an under-construction domain to Google's algorithm.

Business Impact

Google may be depressing the entire domain's rankings. Fix: delete the page, generate and submit a proper sitemap. Recovery in 2–4 weeks.

Finding 1.3
No Meta Descriptions, No H1, No Schema Markup
Critical

Homepage has no meta description, no H1 tag, and no Organization or LocalBusiness schema. Competitors with schema get address and phone shown in Google results. CCW gets a plain blue link.

Finding 1.4
No Contact Info Above the Fold on a $500K+ B2B Site
High

Phone and email require navigating to a separate page. Lowest effort, highest immediate return fix on the entire site.

Finding 1.5
Only 6 Discoverable Pages — No Services Architecture
High

No individual service pages, no fleet-type pages, no case studies, no RFQ flow. Repowers, alternative fuel, ZEPS electric — each needs its own targeted page to rank.

Revenue Impact: $200K–$800K Annual Opportunity Cost

Three press-worthy events in early 2026 — APTA award, RATP Dev contract, new President — drove zero inbound inquiries because the site cannot be crawled. With rendering fixed and proper SEO, each should generate 100–500 qualified procurement contacts per month, indefinitely.

Top 5 Fixes
1
Fix crawlability — generate sitemap, delete "Coming Soon" page

Until Google can read the site, nothing else matters. Est. 2 hours. Results in 2–4 weeks.

2
Add phone + email to every page header

Free. Immediate. The most impactful UX change on the site.

3
Add meta descriptions and H1 tags

Tells Google what each page is about. Improves click-through rate.

4
Implement Organization + LocalBusiness schema

Enables rich results with address and phone in Google search results.

5
Build Services architecture — one page per service

Refurbishing, Repower, Alternative Fuel, Electric Conversions, Parts — each ranked for the searches agencies run when selecting a contractor.

Site 3 — transitsales.com · Confidential
Site 3 of 3 · Highest Score · WordPress + Divi + WooCommerce + Yoast SEO · Riverside, CA
transitsales.com
52/100
Finding 3.1
Homepage Title Is "home - Transit Sales International" — Yoast Misconfigured
Critical

Yoast SEO is installed specifically to prevent this. It has not been configured. Every page earns zero keyword ranking signal. Fix: 10 minutes in Yoast settings. Results in 2–4 weeks.

Finding 3.2
No H1 or H2 Tags on the Homepage — Completely Flat Structure
Critical

Confirmed via live fetch. H1 is the highest-weighted single on-page SEO signal. Google has no structural signal about what this page is for.

Finding 3.3
Fleet Depth Is Invisible — Buyers Think You Have 2 Buses
Critical

TSI stocks 10–15 units per model across 1,000+ total units. The site shows 2 representative listings per model with no unit count, no "View All 14 Available" link, no indication that multiples exist. A transit agency needing 8 identical buses has no way to know TSI can fulfill the order.

Business Impact

The fix is not listing all 1,000 units individually. It's making fleet depth explicit: show "14 available" per model and link to a per-model filtered inventory page. This is the most fixable major conversion issue on the site.

Finding 3.4
Zero Pricing Visible on Any Listing
Critical

Every unit uses YITH Request a Quote — no price, no range. A buyer comparing dealers won't submit a quote form for a $30,000 bus. They click to the competitor who shows a number. Estimated lift from adding pricing: $245K+ annually.

Finding 3.5
No Available/Sold Status — Buyers Can't Trust Any Listing
High

No status indicator on any listing. A buyer who inquires on a unit and learns it sold will not return. Real-time inventory sync is table stakes for a used vehicle dealer.

Finding 3.6
About Page Last Updated March 2021 — 5 Years Stale
High

New President, RATP Dev delivery, TriMet BEV conversion — none reflected. Buyers vetting TSI read a five-year-old page.

The Inventory Is the Product — the Web Presence That Sells It Is Broken

TSI has the fleet depth to fulfill large agency orders. The website makes it look like 2 buses are available. Per-model unit counts, pricing, and availability status would be the highest single-impact change available in 2026. Conservative lift: $245K+ annually — likely 3–5× when fleet depth is properly communicated.
