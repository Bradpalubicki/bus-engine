Critical & High Issues — completecoach.com
Critical No Call-to-Action Above the Fold

Visitors see no clear next step in the first screen. They have to scroll to find out how to contact you. In a high-consideration B2B purchase, every extra scroll loses a decision-maker.

Recommendation: Add a prominent "Request a Quote" or "Contact Us" button visible without scrolling on every page, especially the homepage.
Critical No Content Security Policy Header

Your site has no CSP header. Every form and session is exposed to cross-site scripting injection. Malicious scripts can run silently on any page load.

Recommendation: Configure CSP headers at the server or CDN level. Start with a report-only policy, then lock down to strict-allow lists.
⚖ Federal Impact
NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 SC-18 (Mobile Code) + CMMC Level 1 Access Control. Missing CSP is a finding in any DFARS/CMMC pre-assessment.
Critical HTTPS Enforced But HSTS Header Missing

Browsers can still be downgraded to HTTP by attackers. Without HSTS, a man-in-the-middle can intercept form submissions and contact data.

Recommendation: Add Strict-Transport-Security: max-age=31536000; includeSubDomains to all server responses.
⚖ Federal Impact
CISA Binding Operational Directive 18-01 mandates HSTS for all .gov domains and is the benchmark applied to federal contractor web properties during procurement review.
Critical Accessibility Critically Failing — Section 508 Risk

Inaccessible sites face ADA lawsuits and are excluded from federal contracting opportunities. Multiple WCAG 2.1 AA failures detected including missing alt text, insufficient color contrast, and keyboard navigation gaps.

Recommendation: Conduct a full WCAG 2.1 AA audit. Prioritize: alt text on all images, contrast ratios above 4.5:1, keyboard-navigable menus.
⚖ Federal Impact
Section 508 §1194 of the Rehabilitation Act requires all federal agency vendors to maintain accessible digital properties. Non-compliance is grounds for bid disqualification.
Critical WordPress Site With Unaudited Third-Party Scripts

Unvetted external scripts run on every page, creating supply chain risk. A single compromised plugin or external script can exfiltrate visitor data or inject malicious code for all users.

Recommendation: Audit all third-party plugins and scripts. Remove unused plugins. Implement Subresource Integrity (SRI) for external scripts.
⚖ Federal Impact
CMMC SC.3.177 (Protection of CUI in Systems and Communications) — supply chain risk from unvetted scripts is a direct finding in Level 2+ assessments.
Critical No Federal Contractor Credentials Visible

No CAGE code, NAICS codes, past performance, or SAM.gov registration is visible to procurement officers. A contracting officer who can't verify your eligibility in 30 seconds moves to the next vendor.

Recommendation: Add a dedicated "Government Contracting" section listing CAGE code, NAICS codes, SAM.gov status, and two or three past performance references.
⚖ Federal Impact
FAR 9.104 Responsibility Determination — contracting officers must affirmatively determine a vendor is responsible before award. Missing registration data creates friction that eliminates you from consideration.
High Missing Meta Description

No meta description means Google writes its own snippet — usually a random sentence fragment. This reduces click-through rate from search results by an estimated 5–15%.

Recommendation: Write a 140–160 character meta description for every page. Lead with your strongest value proposition.
High Phone Number Hidden From Header

The phone number is not visible in the header navigation on desktop or mobile. For a high-value B2B service, a visible phone number is a conversion signal — it tells prospects you are real and reachable.

Recommendation: Place a click-to-call phone number in the top-right corner of every page header.
High Form Inputs Missing Accessible Labels

Contact form fields use placeholder text instead of explicit <label> elements. Screen readers cannot identify the fields — a direct Section 508 failure.

Recommendation: Replace all placeholder-only form fields with proper <label for="..."> elements.
High No Past Performance or Portfolio Section

There is no dedicated section showcasing completed projects with outcomes, fleet sizes, or agency names. Federal procurement and large fleet operators want evidence before they call.

Recommendation: Build a structured portfolio: project type, agency/client (or "major transit agency, Pacific Northwest"), scope, completion year. Add 6–10 entries.
