The Core Issue — Two Brands, One Legal Entity
ZEPS is a product brand. Complete Coach Works, Inc. is the legal contracting entity. Every federal contract, every Buy America certificate, every FTA grant application must be signed and submitted by CCW — not ZEPS. This is FAR 4.102. It is not optional.
False Claims Act Exposure — $14,308–$28,619 Per Claim
If a transit agency receives a federal grant and awards a contract to what they believe is "ZEPS" — and CCW's legal entity relationship is not clearly disclosed — there is civil liability exposure under the False Claims Act (31 U.S.C. §§3729–3733). $14,308–$28,619 per false claim plus treble damages (current figure as of July 3, 2025 federal adjustment). This does not require fraudulent intent. Ambiguity is enough.
SAM.gov Gap — Verify Immediately
CCW is registered under UEI QN7UN15K9NP2 (active through August 2026). SAM.gov requires all trade names and DBAs to be listed under the legal registration. If "ZEPS" and "ZEPS Drive" are not listed as trade names in CCW's SAM registration, any bid referencing ZEPS creates a UEI name mismatch — grounds for bid rejection or post-award protest.
Buy America Certificate — Entity Name Must Match
49 CFR §661.12 requires every bid to include a Buy America Certificate signed by the manufacturer of record. The "Company" field must read Complete Coach Works, Inc. — not ZEPS. A certificate signed under the ZEPS brand with CCW's UEI creates a name mismatch. Transit agencies can reject non-conforming bids on responsiveness grounds.
