Critical Issues

What's Broken — and Why It Costs Money

CCW is the flagship — "nation's largest" in its space. The website actively works against that claim at every level.

Finding 1.1
Divi JS Rendering — All Content Invisible to Google
Critical

Divi renders the entire homepage in JavaScript. Google sees a blank HTML shell — no headings, no body text, no semantic structure. CCW's 38 years of expertise, its services, and its credentials are invisible to every search engine.

Business Impact

A transit agency searching "bus refurbishment contractor California" or "ZEPS electric bus conversion" will not find CCW. The nation's largest refurbisher ranks for essentially zero non-branded queries.

Finding 1.2
No Sitemap — "Coming Soon" Zombie Page Is Indexed
Critical

/sitemap.xml returns 404. /wp-sitemap.xml also 404s. A "New Site Coming Soon" page is live, indexed, and likely what Google treats as the primary URL — signaling an under-construction domain to the algorithm.

Business Impact

Google may be depressing the entire domain's rankings. Fix: delete the coming-soon page, generate and submit a proper sitemap. Recovery in 2–4 weeks.

Finding 1.3
No Meta Descriptions, No H1, No Schema Markup Anywhere
Critical

Homepage has no meta description (Google writes its own — usually navigation text or CSS). No H1 in the DOM. No Organization, LocalBusiness, or Service schema. Competitors with schema get address and phone shown directly in search results. CCW gets a plain blue link.

Business Impact

Missing schema and meta descriptions reduce click-through rate 5–15% even when a page does rank. Combined with rendering issues: near-zero organic traffic from any informational query.

Finding 1.4
No Contact Info Above the Fold on a $500K+ B2B Site
High

Phone and email require navigating to a separate page. For a company selling multi-hundred-thousand-dollar contracts to transit agencies, the phone number must be in the header of every page. This is a $0 fix with direct revenue impact.

Business Impact

Every visitor who doesn't already know the number has zero path to becoming a lead. Lowest effort, highest immediate return fix on the entire site.

Finding 1.5
Only 6 Discoverable Pages — No Services Architecture
High

Only 6 pages are indexable: Coming Soon, Refurbishing, About CCW, Sales Team, Leadership, Parts. No individual service pages, no fleet-type pages, no case studies, no RFQ flow. No pages targeting the search terms transit agencies use when selecting a contractor.

Business Impact

Repowers, alternative fuel conversions, ZEPS battery electric, parts — each needs its own targeted page with a quote CTA. These pages don't exist and won't rank until they do.

Finding 1.6
Active Blog Generating Zero Search Value
Medium

Blog is active (last post March 5, 2026). APTA award win, RATP Dev contract, new President — all newsworthy. None drives search traffic because rendering blocks indexing and there are no target keywords, internal links, or CTAs on any post.

Business Impact

With rendering fixed and proper SEO, each announcement could drive 50–200 qualified procurement contacts per month, indefinitely.

Revenue Impact
Conservative Annual Opportunity Cost: $200K–$800K in Lost Inbound Contracts

CCW wins zero leads through organic search. Three press-worthy events in early 2026 drove zero inbound inquiries because the site cannot be crawled. Each, properly SEO'd, should generate 100–500 relevant visitors per month indefinitely — exactly the procurement staff CCW wants to reach.

Automation Opportunities
📋
RFQ Pipeline Automation

Intake form (fleet size, bus type, service, timeline) → auto-assign to regional rep → CRM entry + email confirmation. Currently 100% phone-based with no digital trail.

📣
Contract Win Syndication

New contract wins auto-post to LinkedIn + trigger email blast to agency procurement contacts. Every RATP Dev-level win should drive more than a single blog post.

🔩
Parts Inquiry Routing

Self-serve parts request form with auto-acknowledgment and estimated lead time. Eliminates routine inbound phone volume for standard parts requests.

🔗
Cross-Company Lead Routing

CCW inquiry needing leasing → auto-introduction to SBL. Buyer wanting to purchase → routed to TSI. Currently all manual, likely lost.

Top 5 Fixes — Priority Order
1
Fix crawlability — generate /wp-sitemap.xml, submit to Search Console, delete "Coming Soon" page

Until Google can read the site, nothing else matters. Est. 2 hours. Results visible in 2–4 weeks.

2
Add phone number and email to the header of every page

Free. Immediate. The most impactful UX change on the site for any B2B buyer.

3
Add meta descriptions and H1 tags to every key page

Tells Google what each page is about. Improves click-through rate in search results.

4
Implement Organization + LocalBusiness schema with phone, address, social links

Enables rich results — address and phone shown in the SERP. Competitors have this. CCW does not.

5
Build a Services architecture — one page per service with targeted keywords and a quote CTA

Refurbishing, Repower, Alternative Fuel, Electric Conversions, Parts — each page ranks for the searches agencies run when selecting a contractor.

Critical Issues

Yoast Installed But Not Configured — Inventory UX Needs Rethinking

TSI scores highest because Yoast and a functional sitemap exist. But Yoast is misconfigured on every key page, and the way inventory is presented doesn't match how the business actually works.

Finding 3.1
Homepage Title Is "home - Transit Sales International" — Yoast Misconfigured
Critical

The word "home" leads the title tag — the single most important on-page SEO signal and the headline Google shows in every search result. Yoast SEO is installed specifically to prevent this. It has not been configured. Should read: "Transit Sales International | Used Buses for Sale | Riverside, CA"

Business Impact

The homepage earns zero keyword ranking signal for "used buses for sale" or any commercial query. Fix in 10 minutes in Yoast. Results in 2–4 weeks.

Finding 3.2
No H1 on the Homepage — No H2 Tags Either
Critical

Confirmed: no H1 in the DOM, no H2 tags. The page is structurally flat. H1 is the highest-weighted single on-page SEO signal. Without it, Google has no structural indicator of what the page is for.

Finding 3.3
Inventory Display Shows Only 2 Units Per Model — By Design, But Misrepresented
Critical

TSI has 1,000+ units across roughly 10–15 models. The site shows 2 representative listings per model — that's the intended design, not a data problem. But the execution fails: there is no "View All 15 Available 40ft Gillig" link, no count shown per model, no way for a buyer to know that 10–15 more identical units exist. The site reads as if only 2 buses are available in the entire inventory.

The fix is not to list all 1,000 units individually. It's to make the model-based system explicit: show "14 available" next to each model, link to a filtered inventory page per model, and communicate that TSI stocks multiples of each unit.

Business Impact

A transit agency needing 8 buses of the same model needs to know TSI can fulfill that order. If the site shows 2 listings with no indication of depth, they call a competitor who makes their fleet depth obvious. This is the most fixable major conversion issue on the site.

Finding 3.4
Zero Pricing Visible on Any Listing
Critical

Every listing uses YITH Request a Quote — no price, no range, no "starting at." A buyer comparing dealers from search results will not submit a quote form for a $30,000 bus. They click to the competitor who shows a number.

Business Impact

Estimated lift from adding pricing: $245K+ annually based on current visible inventory alone. Across the full fleet depth this multiplies significantly.

Finding 3.5
No Available/Sold Status — Buyers Can't Trust Any Listing
High

No status indicator on any listing — Available, Pending, or Sold. For a high-turnover used vehicle dealer, a buyer who inquires on a unit and learns it sold will not come back. Listing status is table stakes for any used vehicle marketplace.

Business Impact

Real-time sync between internal inventory system and website status is the single most important technical infrastructure investment for TSI. Without it, every listing is a trust liability.

Finding 3.6
About Page Last Updated March 2021 — 5 Years Stale
High

New President, RATP Dev delivery, TriMet BEV conversion milestone — none reflected. A buyer vetting TSI as a vendor reads a five-year-old About page and draws their own conclusions about whether the company is active.

Revenue Impact
The Inventory Is the Product — the Web Presence That Sells It Is Broken

TSI has the fleet depth to fulfill large agency orders. The website makes it look like they have 2 buses for sale. Adding per-model unit counts, pricing, and availability status would be the highest single-impact change available to TSI in 2026. Conservative lift estimate: $245K+ annually, likely 3–5× that when fleet depth is communicated properly.
