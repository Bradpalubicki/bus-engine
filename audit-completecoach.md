# Website Audit — completecoach.com
**Complete Coach Works | The Nation's Largest Refurbishing, Retrofit and Repair Company**
Platform: WordPress + Divi 4.27.6 | ESOP company | 38+ years | Riverside, CA
Audit Date: 2026-03-17

---

## Overall Score: 48/100

---

## Critical Issues

1. **No crawlable content** — Divi renders everything in JavaScript. Google sees a nearly blank page. The company claiming "nation's largest" is invisible in search.
2. **No sitemap** — `/sitemap.xml` returns 404. A "Coming Soon" zombie page is live and indexed.
3. **No meta description** on the homepage — Google writes its own, usually garbage.
4. **No schema markup** — competitors get rich results (address, phone, hours in Google). CCW gets nothing.
5. **Phone number and email buried** — no contact info above the fold on a site selling $500K+ contracts.
6. **Blog is active but disconnected** — APTA award win, RATP Dev contract, new President appointment — all generating zero search traffic because none of it is properly structured for Google.
7. **Only 6 discoverable pages** — no services hub, no case studies, no fleet types, no RFQ flow.

---

## Revenue Impact

A transit agency procurement manager searching "bus refurbishment contractor" or "ZEPS electric bus conversion" won't find CCW. Three major press events in early 2026 drove zero search traffic because the site can't be crawled.

**Conservative opportunity cost: $200K–$800K/year in lost inbound contracts.**

---

## Automation Opportunities

- RFQ intake form → auto-assign to regional sales rep + CRM entry
- New contract wins → auto-post to LinkedIn + email blast to agency procurement contacts
- Parts inquiry form with auto-acknowledgment (eliminates phone volume)

---

## Top 5 Fixes (Priority Order)

1. Fix crawlability — generate and submit `/wp-sitemap.xml`, delete the "Coming Soon" zombie page
2. Add meta descriptions to every page
3. Add Organization + LocalBusiness schema with phone, address, NAP consistency
4. Put phone number and email in the header on every page
5. Build a proper Services architecture with individual pages per service line (each with a quote CTA)
